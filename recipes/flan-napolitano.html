Flan Napolitano

Description:

Flan napolitano is a popular Mexican take on the classic flan or crème caramel dessert. It's creaminess and sweetness makes it a favorite in the country. The ingredients needed to make this dessert are easy to find, and the steps to follow are fairly simple.

If you have a sweet tooth, you won't regret making it!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of white sugar
  • 1 can of condensed milk
  • 1 can of evaporated milk
  • 6 medium eggs
  • 1 cream cheese bar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Steps:

  1. Preheat your oven at 180 °C (356 °F) for 10-15 minutes.
  2. In a aluminum oven mould or flanera, put the white sugar cup and heat it until it becomes a golden liquid. This will be our caramel, so be careful not to burn it. Reserve.
  3. Mix all the ingredients in a blender and pour your blend inside the aluminum mould with the caramel and cover it with tin foil.
  4. Put the mould in an oven-proof pyrex glass roaster with warm water. The water must reach half of the aluminum mould. Depending of your oven power, bake around 45 minutes or 1 hour.
  5. Take it out from the oven and let it cool enough to put it inside the fridge. Let it sit for around 4 hours or overnight in the fridge.
  6. Cut in slices and serve. Enjoy!
